# Event And Community Accessibility

Use this reference when the target is an event, meeting, seminar, workshop, community operation, community onboarding, public participation process, or political/civic information flow.

## Review Frame

Review before, during, and after the event or community interaction. Accessibility is not only the event day; it starts with finding the opportunity and continues through archive, follow-up, and rejoining later.

## Before

- Publish purpose, audience, date/time, location/URL, costs, language, agenda, required apps, archive policy, and contact path.
- Explain venue route, elevator, restroom, seating, quiet/recovery space, child/family considerations, streaming availability, and recording policy.
- Ask about support needs with examples, deadline, privacy boundary, and what can/cannot be provided.
- Prepare captioning, sign-language interpretation, microphone rules, chat/Q&A path, printed materials, and staff handoff.
- For live captions or speech-to-text support, define the tool category, audio input path, editor role, participant access route such as QR/link, backup, and correction policy.
- For sign-language interpretation, define the request route, lead time, duration, number of interpreters, cost/contract owner, prep materials, terminology alignment, placement, lighting, camera framing, break/rotation plan, and archive permission.
- For public or political contexts, identify legal, venue, contract, campaign, recording, signage, and distribution constraints before promising support.

## During

- State the agenda, participation rules, support paths, recording/caption policy, and how to ask questions.
- Repeat audience questions into the microphone or chat before answering.
- Keep speaker face, sign interpreter, captions, and shared materials visible and well framed.
- Avoid rapid screen changes, unexplained visuals, color-only cues, and unannounced chat-only decisions.
- Keep private support information limited to staff who need it.

## After

- Publish summary, transcript, corrected captions, slide/material links, decisions, next actions, and contact path in a findable place.
- Preserve the archive location and version/date so late participants can catch up.
- Record what support worked, what failed, and what should become a default for the next event.

## Community And Supporter Information Flow

- Provide a newcomer path to the right communication channel, calendar, portal, wiki, or file location.
- When using a portal or wiki, state who it is for, whether it is public or team-only, what may be shared externally, how updates happen, and where confused users should ask for help.
- Separate urgent notices, reference material, decisions, tasks, and discussion so people can recover later.
- Explain roles, permissions, terminology, meeting cadence, and where to ask for help.
- Keep essential instructions available as a plain-text overview with an access request path when a calendar, portal, or login-only page is involved.
- Avoid insider abbreviations and channel-only knowledge for essential participation.
- Keep summaries and next actions available outside synchronous meetings.
